Category: Fossil Fuel Activities (Non-Taxonomy-Aligned)
EU Taxonomy Objective: None — explicitly excluded from taxonomy-aligned activities
NACE Code Reference: B05.10 (Mining of hard coal), B06.10 (Extraction of crude petroleum), B06.20 (Extraction of natural gas), C19.20 (Manufacture of refined petroleum products), G46.71 (Wholesale of solid, liquid and gaseous fuels)

Definition:
Economic activities involving the extraction, production, processing, distribution, or combustion of fossil fuels including coal, crude oil, diesel, petrol, LPG, heavy fuel oil, and natural gas. These activities are not taxonomy-aligned under any EU Taxonomy objective and represent brown expenditure that contributes to greenhouse gas emissions. In GreenLedger, these activities reduce the Green Credit Score as they represent direct fossil fuel dependency in the SME's procurement spend.

Non-qualifying activities (explicitly brown):
- Purchase of diesel, petrol, or kerosene for vehicles, forklifts, generators, or heating
- Purchase of LPG or propane for heating, processing, or industrial applications
- Purchase of coal or coal-derived fuels for heat treatment, firing, or energy generation
- Coal-fired furnace operation, servicing, or maintenance contracts
- Natural gas supply contracts and related equipment servicing (where primary use is combustion)
- Fuel oil purchases for boilers, dryers, or process heating
- Carbon-intensive freight and transport services powered by fossil fuels
- Maintenance and repair of fossil-fuel-powered machinery or combustion equipment

Example invoice descriptions:
diesel fuel, diesel B7, petrol, gasoline, LPG, propane, butane, coal, lignite, fuel oil, heavy fuel oil, kerosene, natural gas, fossil fuel, coal-fired, furnace fuel, heating oil, gas oil, forklift fuel, generator fuel, coal furnace service, oil boiler, gas boiler, combustion fuel, fossil energy

Do substantial harm considerations:
N/A — these activities are not eligible for EU Taxonomy alignment. Any SME expenditure in this category counts as carbon-intensive spend and is reflected negatively in sustainability dimension scoring. Lenders using GreenLedger should treat a high proportion of fossil fuel procurement spend as a risk factor when assessing green creditworthiness.
